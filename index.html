Academic Biography

I am a dedicated doctoral researcher working alongside Advisor Prof. Sadagopan Krishnan. My scientific mission centers around scaling fundamental micro-electrochemical systems into versatile translation platforms—particularly "Point of Care" (POC) diagnostics and integrated wearable bio-sensors.

My multi-disciplinary toolkit bridges organic-inorganic material configurations, custom microfabrication routines, and quantitative physical metrics to drive safety, continuity, and intelligence across consumer diagnostics, clinical monitoring, and agricultural surveillance systems.

Research Overview & Projects

⚡

Multiplexed Microneedle Biosensors

Engineering electrochemical, skin-interfaced microneedle diagnostic arrays capable of evaluating multiple key physiological biomarkers inside Interstitial Fluid (ISF) concurrently without classic clinical boundaries.

🎨

Colorimetric Biosensing Arrays

Developing instrument-free, chemical visual assays designed to process broad-spectrum ambient tests natively across decentralised clinical, environmental, or agricultural setups.

🧬

Molecularly Imprinted Polymers (MIPs)

Synthesizing biomimetic matrix structures acting as scalable synthetic antibodies, maximizing diagnostic hardware lifecycle robustly across remote environments.

🔋

Energy Micro-Conversion

Investigating materials and pathways configured specifically to harvest or transform energy micro-scales directly on-device to power self-sustaining diagnostics ecosystems.

Latest Project & Publication Highlight

Electrochemical Strip Sensor Based on a Graphene-Au-PEDOT Nanocomposite

Developing highly specialized, integrated matrix sensors designed for continuous monitoring and trace-level tracking of Dacarbazine alongside translational therapeutics.
